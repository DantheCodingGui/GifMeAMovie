this documentary part of american experience 1988 qv series examines the events leading up to what is now seen as the defining moment in the establishment of the gay rights movement in the united states the riot at the stonewall inn in new york city in the summer of 1969 at that time homosexuality was not only illegal it was classified as mental illness bars like stonewall were controlled by the mob and the police were paid to either look the other way or conduct their raids early in the day on this night however the police arrived when the bar was full the reaction was swift with crowds quickly forming outside the bar the next night a crowd estimated in the thousands again confronted the police as a result of these actions the gay community made themselves known for the first time a year later in the summer of 1970 many of those involved staged the first gay pride parade
