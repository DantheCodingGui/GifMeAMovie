in the last one hundred years lake eyre has filled totally only four times it is a marvellous spectacle as the normally arid salt basin fills with water and blooms with new life someone once described the experience as being like trekking across the sahara and falling into the mediterranean this weeks australian story looks at the lake through the eyes of the people of william creek the nearest settlement the main focus is publican mal anderson and his family theyre melbournians who moved to william creek three years ago after visiting the area and falling in love with it mal a mechanical engineer made an offer for the pub and moved west along with his wife children and grandchildren we also meet phil gee a former winemaker who moved to the creek ten years ago with his zoologist wife they consult on conservation issues and run camel safari tours into the desert
