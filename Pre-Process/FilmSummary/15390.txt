as one of australias first indigenous doctors mark wenitong combines his skills as a physician and a musician to fight ill health in far north queensland including a looming ice epidemic his greatest role model was his mother lealon who in the 1950s and 60s fought against the odds to become a pioneering indigenous health worker despite his dedication to his medical work mark wenitong describes himself as primarily a musician a talent he has passed on to all his children daughter naomi rose to prominence as half of successful duo shakaya while eldest son joel is also a successful musician but a terrible car crash plunged the family into crisis naomi nearly died and the trauma for joel whod been driving led him to follow in his fathers footsteps and become a doctor australian story follows the wenitongs as they continue to work in music and medicine within both the indigenous and wider community
